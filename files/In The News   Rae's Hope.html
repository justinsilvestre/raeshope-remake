Rae’s Hope and PAL Launch Joint Effort

Posted on February 16, 2014 by Rae's Hope

Comments

0

Rae’s Hope and PAL Launch Joint Effort

Rae’s Hope guides girls through adolescence in many ways, including teaching them that creating partnerships and building healthy relationships are essential to living a fulfilling life.

This is true for Rae’s Hope itself as well!  We live this by forging alliances with other community agencies to deliver long-term, constructive programming to at-risk girls in Dallas. 

Rae’s Hope is proud to announce that it has launched a joint effort with the Police Athletic League (PAL) of Dallas, effective January 2014 at 6pm on Thursday evenings throughout the school year at the BethlehemCenter located at 4410 Leland Avenue, Dallas, TX75215.

PAL is a youth-crime prevention program that uses educational, athletic, and recreational activities to create trust and understanding between police officers and young people in the community, on a neighborhood-by-neighborhood basis, one person at a time. 

PAL believes that young people, if they are reached early enough, experience constructive interactions, and get to know officers as good, concerned individuals, can develop strong positive attitudes towards law and order in their journey through life toward the goal of maturity and good citizenship.

The girls in Rae’s Hope are learning lessons about life, about how they can build a great future for themselves, and about how effort to overcome challenges and achieve self-reliance needs to be part of their daily goals – lessons they may not receive anywhere else.  These values and approaches match PAL’ s aims and influences perfectly.  From a platform of fitness activities, Rae’s Hope delivers lessons in Life Skills, Nutrition & Healthy Living, and Financial Literacy to young girls in the South and West Dallas communities.

Parents see that PAL’s involvement creates a safer environment for their children as well as the neighborhood.  Mothers and fathers comment that they love the opportunities their children have to experience, first-hand, additional positive adult male and female role models. 

Don’t miss out!  Arrive a little before 6:00pm on Thursday evenings, ready for weekly fun, sportsmanship, life skills, and the opportunity to be part of a lasting, constructive, supportive environment.

 

For more information, contact Angela Fields at:  angelafields52@gmail.com

Each year, the Dallas Mavericks provide Rae’s Hope with tickets for our girls to see a Maverick’s game.  This year,  over 50 parents and their daughters enjoyed seeing the Mavericks beat the Atlanta Hawks in a preseason game on October 23.   Everyone had a great time.  Over 50% of this year’s group had never been to a Maverick’s game.   Rae’s Hope is proud to be able to give our girls and their parents this great experience and we thank the Mavericks for their generosity in helping us make it happen.

Update from the Executive Director

Posted on October 19, 2013 by Rae's Hope

Comments

0

Dear Friends of Rae’s Hope,

Our Fall programs are off to a great start.    Our outreach has expanded to 50 girls and we’ve added a second venue, the Lakewest YMCA, in addition to the Park South “Y”.  We’re very grateful to the Lakewest YMCA for enabling us to bring our programs to girls in their area.

Our classroom instruction has covering topics such as Communication, Friendship and Self Awareness.  Classes on Financial Literacy, Nutrition, Reading and Healthy Living are scheduled for rotation through the end of the year. 

Sharon Roberts of Dallas CEO Netweavers has been a wonderful new volunteer this fall.  Sharon has been teaching a writing class to girls on Wednesday evenings called Pivotal People.  The girls are writing about people that have been pivotal in their lives and Sharon is using the experience to help them strengthen their writing skills.  Thank you Sharon for your time and effort in working with our girls!  Volunteers like you really help us deliver better learning experiences for our girls.

Speaking of volunteers, we need more!  If you’re interested in volunteering as a classroom instructor, volleyball coach or life skills instructor we need you!  Please fill out our volunteer form under the Forms tab on the Rae’s Hope web site.

On Oct. 23rd we’ll be taking up to 50 girls to the Maverick’s game.  We try to do this at least once very year and it’s always a fun night.  Thanks to the Mavericks for their generosity in providing these tickets every year.

Finally, thank you all for your generous donations to Rae’s Hope   Thanks to you we’re able to expand our programs to more girls in more locations, and we hope to continue provide more girls with a better vision for their future.

Angela
